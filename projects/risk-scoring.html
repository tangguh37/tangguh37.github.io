Problem

Banks need to assess loan default risk for customers, but traditional scoring models are often black boxes. This project builds a transparent, tiered risk scoring system that classifies customers into risk levels — enabling lenders to make informed decisions.

Final project for the Data Science Bootcamp (Don Quixote cohort), selected as the best group project.

What I Built

As Project Lead, I led the end-to-end execution: strategic planning, task scheduling, decision-making, and team direction. Built and compared multiple classification models, engineered features from raw loan data, and implemented a tiered scoring system that maps prediction probabilities to interpretable risk levels (Low → Medium → High → Very High).

Learnings

Leading a team project taught me that clear communication about model trade-offs is as important as technical implementation. The tiered approach makes ML accessible to non-technical stakeholders — they don't need to understand log-odds, they just need to know which tier a customer falls into.
